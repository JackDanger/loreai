* **Always prefer delete-and-recompute over invalidation/validation logic for stale cached state**: When cached state (like a frozen \`insertAt\` index) can become stale due to underlying data reshuffling, the user rejects approaches that validate, patch, or re-derive from the stale state. The design principle: no state to track or invalidate, no drift possible, cleaner invariants. When proposing fixes for stale cached positions or indices, always suggest delete-on-compress with same-turn recomputation rather than validity checks or conditional re-derivation.
